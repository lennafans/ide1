  • Results of municipality election 2017.
    This infographic contains an abundance of information. It is able to capture both the destinction between municipality/region elections, geographical dispersion and vote distribution. Using a map to display the actual municipalities and colour codes to signal the overall results of the individual locations, without the user having to navigate into details. By clicking and interacting on the map, a view of regions, municipalities and later constituencies, explains the vote distribution with a high degree of detail. Especially the historical comparison (percentage- and candidate-wise) is useful for putting the information in context. On the negative side it contains a lot of information that inevitable is confusing to navigate. It is also a little sluggish, discouraging the user to thoroughly interact with all levels in the visualisation.
  • A visual introduction to machine learning.
    This website uses visualization to explain some of the main concepts in machine learning in a simple and intuitive way. The whole page works as an interactive visualization where the information changes as the reader scrolls through the page. While some of the effects might seem a bit excessive, the animations help convey some rather complex ideas and concepts in an intuitive and interactive way.Since the target audience is most likely people with little or no knowledge of machine learning, the website might benefit from being even more interactive. For example letting the user change some of the variables in the process would provide a better experience through hands-on experience with the area. But again, more interactivity might also clutter the visualization and make the experience more confusing.

Jens Egholm

I work with neuromorphic computation and machine learning, and through that data with a high degree of complexity. I often get the feeling that I miss important correlations and hard-to-see causalities, simply because the data is difficult to intuit about. Focusing on the neuromorphic part, I would get the data from different 3d model datasets and the Human Brain Project, who are currently storing terrabytes of data on neural connections and neural models of humans, rats and mice.

To begin with it would be interestconfusinging to visualise some of the connections of the different brain modules, to underline the complexity of the domain. The story of such a visualisation would focus on giving the user an understanding of the diffent areas of the brain, how they are connected and potentially how they work. This would bring some of the at times inaccessible information to a broader audience. But the primary audience would be the researchers associated with the project, to help them visualising information to shed light on otherwise hidden aspects of the data.
