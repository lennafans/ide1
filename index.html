Jens Egholm

I work with neuromorphic computation and machine learning, and through that data with a high degree of complexity. I often get the feeling that I miss important correlations and hard-to-see causalities, simply because the data is difficult to intuit about. Focusing on the neuromorphic part, I would get the data from different 3d model datasets and the Human Brain Project, who are currently storing terrabytes of data on neural connections and neural models of humans, rats and mice.

To begin with it would be interesting to visualise some of the connections of the different brain modules, to underline the complexity of the domain. The story of such a visualisation would focus on giving the user an understanding of the diffent areas of the brain, how they are connected and potentially how they work. This would bring some of the at times inaccessible information to a broader audience. But the primary audience would be the researchers associated with the project, to help them visualising information to shed light on otherwise hidden aspects of the data.

This first prototype takes the .stl file and imports it using threejs. The model is downloaded from the https://senselab.med.yale.edu/3dmodeldb database and describes an Olfactory bulb mitral cell microcurcuit in a rat.
